# Customer-churn-and-Risk-mitigation
An analysis carried out to provide a data-driven evaluation of customer retention and operational risk. By utilizing an open-source dataset, a comprehensive analytical framework was constructed to identify the underlying drivers of customer attrition and to categorize service-level performance.
Project Objectives:
The primary focus of this analysis was the transformation of raw customer interaction data into actionable insights. A dual-layered approach was adopted, where historical performance was balanced against predictive risk indicators to facilitate proactive management.
Key Analytical Components:
The following areas were prioritized and visualized within the dashboard:
•	Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): High-level metrics, such as total customer volume, average tenure, and service satisfaction scores, were aggregated to establish a baseline for business health.
•	Churn Distribution: Patterns of customer exit were analysed across various demographics and service types to pinpoint where the highest rates of turnover occurred.
•	Customer Risk Assessment: A risk-scoring mechanism was developed, wherein customers were categorized based on behavioural triggers such as low engagement, frequent support tickets, and declining usage rates.
•	Financial Impact Analysis: The potential revenue loss associated with at-risk segments was calculated, allowing for the prioritization of retention efforts.
Methodology:
1.	Data Acquisition: An open-source dataset containing customer profiles, contract details, and interaction history was sourced.
2.	Data Cleaning and Transformation: Null values were addressed, and categorical variables were encoded to ensure a robust foundation for risk modeling.
3.	Risk Categorization: Customers were segmented into "Low," "Medium," and "High" risk tiers based on a weighted analysis of satisfaction and contract stability.
4.	Visual Synthesis: An interactive interface was designed to allow for the seamless filtering of data by region, contract type, and risk level.
Conclusion:
From this project, it was demonstrated that churn is rarely the result of a single event but rather a culmination of unresolved friction points. By visualizing these risks systematically, a clear path toward improved customer loyalty and stabilized revenue was established.
Project Specifications
•	Data Source: Open-source Customer Retention Dataset
•	Focus Areas: Churn Prediction, Risk Segmentation, KPI Tracking
